Data Starter Kit

Human-induced climate change is predicted to increase the frequency and intensity of climate and weather extremes causing widespread, adverse impacts to infrastructure, settlements, people, and ecosystems. Geospatial data are fundamental for conducting climate risk analyses, which are necessary to inform policy decisions aimed at reducing risk. Yet, access to data is often a barrier to climate risk modelling, particularly in developing countries. Data are oftentimes scattered, hard to find, in formats that are difficult to use or require considerable technical expertise. This country-specific 'starter kit' aims to facilitate access to relevant geospatial datasets to kickstart the process and act as a starting point for further model development and analysis.
